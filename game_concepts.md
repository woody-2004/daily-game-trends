## Run: 2026-06-23

*Rubric: Viral Potential (V), Buildability (B), Market Gap (G), Retention Strength (R), each 1–10. Score = sum /40.*

### Concept 1 (Safe): "Tideglass" — Score: 28/40

**Score breakdown:** V6 | B8 | G6 | R8

**Core loop:** Grow and breed rare fish/coral in your aquarium; fish grow even while offline. Sell rare specimens, reinvest in tanks and breeding combos.

**Progression:** Common → mythic rarity ladder; tank-size and breeding-slot upgrades.

**Social mechanics:** Visit friends' aquariums; once per day you can "net" one unguarded fish from an undefended tank (mirrors the proven Steal-a-Brainrot/Grow-a-Garden-2 raid layer).

**Monetization:** Gacha currency for rare fish, cosmetic tanks/decor, limited-edition seasonal fish drops.

**Retention hooks:** Offline-growth check-in compulsion + raid loss-aversion (don't let your prize fish get netted) + daily login food bonus.

**Emotional trigger:** Nurturing/collecting blended with loss-aversion.

**Why now:** Directly reuses the two highest-confidence mechanics at launch (idle/offline progression, steal/raid loss-aversion) in an unclaimed visual theme (aquatic) instead of the saturated plant/creature theme.

**AI-buildable?** Yes. Idle growth + async raid does not require real-time multiplayer infrastructure. Realistic 90-day solo build.

### Concept 2 (Aggressive): "Brainrot Olympics" — Score: 28/40

**Score breakdown:** V9 | B5 | G7 | R7

**Core loop:** Collect absurd AI-generated meme creatures; periodic live timed "Olympic" mini-events (tug-of-war, races) where creatures compete for spectators; raid mechanic lets you steal unguarded creatures between events.

**Progression:** Creatures gain power tied to real-world meme-virality scores — content pipeline must track trending memes and refresh creature roster.

**Social mechanics:** Live concurrent events designed for mass-CCU spikes (modeled on Steal a Brainrot's 12.8M-CCU Bruno Mars concert) and clip-friendly absurd animation for TikTok.

**Retention hooks:** Live-event FOMO, raid loss-aversion, meme-relevance refresh keeps content evergreen.

**Emotional trigger:** Humor/absurdity + tribal status (whose creature wins) + FOMO.

**Risk:** Requires ongoing live-ops and a meme-trend-chasing content pipeline — high operational overhead, NOT realistically solo-buildable.

### Concept 3 (AI-Native): "Hearth" — Score: 29/40

**Score breakdown:** V7 | B5 | G9 | R8

**Core loop:** Assign a persistent-memory AI agent with a personality to manage a village/farm/base. While you're offline it autonomously makes decisions (defend, trade, expand) based on goals you set; on return it narrates a story-log of what happened and asks for new directives.

**Progression:** The agent's "trust/skill" grows as it learns your preferences — personalization deepens over time, raising emotional attachment and switching cost.

**Social mechanics:** Share AI-curated highlight clips ("what my agent did today") — comedic/dramatic moments comparable to friends' agents.

**Monetization:** Subscription for deeper agent behaviors/more compute; cosmetic agent personas.

**Retention hooks:** Curiosity about what happened while away (asynchronous idle hook, stronger than plain idle growth because outcomes are non-deterministic and narrated) + genuine emotional bond with a "living" character that remembers you.

**Emotional trigger:** Companionship + anticipation.

**Why now:** Targets the "AI agent as core gameplay loop" white space — large macro market signal, zero breakout consumer title yet.

**Flag:** A scoped-down text-based MVP (simple village sim + single-LLM-call agent loop) is realistically buildable solo with AI coding tools within 90 days, even though the full vision is bigger.

### Concept 4 (Solo-Developer): "Pocket Raid" — Score: 25/40

**Score breakdown:** V5 | B9 | G5 | R6

**Core loop:** Grow a small idle garden/shop around one core resource; async daily raid against other players' stored-state plots (no real-time multiplayer infra needed).

**Progression:** Intentionally narrow — 4–5 rarity tiers, one upgrade tree, no breeding system.

**Social mechanics:** Async leaderboard + a simple "send a friend a gift" mechanic.

**Monetization:** Rewarded ads + one-time "remove ads / double growth speed" IAP.

**Retention hooks:** Push notification when growth completes + daily raid cooldown.

**Emotional trigger:** Nurturing + light loss-aversion, deliberately low cognitive load.

**Why now:** Reuses the two highest-confidence mechanics (idle progression, raid loss-aversion) but trims scope hard enough for a single AI-assisted developer to actually ship.

**Flag:** Most realistically solo-AI-buildable in under 90 days — simple stack (web/React + Firebase or equivalent BaaS), no novel tech risk.

## Run: 2026-06-24

### Concept 1 (Safe/Hybrid): "Settlement of Spires" — Score: 30/40

**Score breakdown:** V7 | B6 | G8 | R9

**Pitch:** A cozy roguelike deckbuilder (Slay the Spire 2 / Islantiles lineage) where each run also grows a small settlement, and — natively, not as a bolted-on stream overlay — a "town hall vote" mechanic lets a linked audience (TikTok/Discord) vote on key run decisions (which card to take, which building to place, fight vs. flee).

**Why now:**
- Rides the #1 confidence mechanic (roguelike deckbuilding) and cozy/systems hybrids.
- Fills market gaps (cozy + strategy, all-ages; purpose-built TikTok-native interactive mechanic) simultaneously.
- Differentiates from Islantiles/Slay the Spire 2 by making the audience-vote loop a first-class feature rather than a streaming add-on.

**Core loop:** Run a deck-based roguelike dungeon crawl → earn resources → expand a cozy settlement between runs → optionally open "vote mode," where viewers pick the next card draft or building placement in real time.

**Risk:** Audience-vote mechanic is unproven as a core (not gimmick) feature — needs a single-player mode that's fully satisfying without an audience.

**AI-buildable?** Partially. Deckbuilder core is achievable in 90 days with AI tools. Full settlement + vote integration stretches toward 6 months.

### Concept 2 (Aggressive): "Corporate Mole" — Score: 28/40

**Score breakdown:** V9 | B5 | G8 | R6

**Pitch:** A social deception hide-and-seek game set in a hyper-realistic open-plan office. One player is a robot infiltrator who must mimic human NPC behavior — walk at the right pace, attend the right meetings, respond to "hey!" exactly like a distracted coworker. Human players watch the floor and try to catch anything that moves slightly wrong. All cosmetics tradeable on Steam Market.

**Why now:**
- MECCHA CHAMELEON proved the camouflage hide-and-seek formula generates more Twitch viewership than VALORANT.
- Office setting is more universally relatable and funnier — higher virality potential.
- Steam Market cosmetics create retention through an economy layer (validated by TBH: Task Bar Hero).

**Core loop:** 5–10 min rounds → robot blends in with AI NPCs → humans observe for tells → correct ID = humans win; successful meeting attended = robot wins → cosmetic drop.

**Risk:** Multiplayer lobby infrastructure is hard to build solo. Consider "AI NPCs fill empty slots" fallback.

---

### Concept 3 (AI-Native): "The Rogue Protocol" — Score: 30/40

**Score breakdown:** V6 | B7 | G9 | R8

**Pitch:** A cyberpunk heist game where your partner is a rogue AI agent (powered by an actual LLM) that has its own goals, memory, and willingness to betray or cooperate depending on how you treat it. Roguelike structure; each run the AI starts fresh but with personality seeds carried from meta-progression.

**Why now:**
- Claude/GPT APIs make a 5-minute heist session with 10–20 AI turns economically viable (cents per session).
- No game has shipped AI-agent-as-core-partner where the AI's reasoning is genuine, not scripted. Market gap: 9/10.
- Clair Obscur Expedition 33 confirms players want games where every interaction feels meaningful — AI reasoning creates genuine unpredictability instead of RNG.

**Core loop:** Agree on heist plan → execute with AI partner calling live decisions → AI may improvise, betray, or self-sacrifice based on relationship state → debrief affects next run's personality weights.

**AI-buildable?** Yes. Heist logic is simple (room-by-room state flags); AI partner is the complexity. 90-day MVP realistic.

### Concept 4 (Solo-Developer): "Taskbar Dungeon" — Score: 29/40

**Score breakdown:** V7 | B9 | G6 | R7

**Pitch:** A tiny auto-battler dungeon crawler in the Windows system tray / macOS menu bar. Monochrome 1-bit pixel art. Free to play. Differentiators vs. TBH: Task Bar Hero: (1) macOS support; (2) lightweight audience mechanic — stream viewers emoji-vote on next dungeon branch; (3) no Steam Market (pure fun framing).

**Why now:**
- TBH: Task Bar Hero at 445K CCU validates "runs-alongside-your-work-day" idle RPG format.
- Audience-vote mechanic upgraded to High confidence post-MECCHA CHAMELEON.
- Realistic 4–6 week MVP; 90 days for polished 1.0.

**Core loop:** Pixel heroes auto-battle in tiny window → at floor completion, vote on next branch → every 30 min: loot drop window. No mandatory engagement.

**AI-buildable?** Yes — most AI-buildable concept on the board. Auto-battler = simple state machines; 1-bit art = fast to generate.
