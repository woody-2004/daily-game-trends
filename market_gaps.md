# Market Gaps — 2026-06-24

1. **Cozy + light-strategy crossover, all-ages.** Cozy gaming audiences are large and underserved on content platforms relative to competitive gaming, but few studios are building dedicated cozy/strategy hybrids beyond a handful of breakout titles (Islantiles). Room for more entrants before the niche saturates.
2. **Casual and educational games for non-core demographics.** Industry investment concentrates on high-budget releases or persistent online worlds; there's no comprehensive ecosystem serving players across all life stages (kids, older adults, casual-only players).
3. **TikTok-native interactive games.** Decision-based livestream mechanics are trending on content platforms but largely exist as streaming gimmicks bolted onto existing games, not as purpose-built products — open lane for a game designed natively around audience-voted decisions.
4. **Mid-budget niche genres outside the big franchises.** Sports, racing, and fighting genres are franchise-locked, but adjacent under-explored angles (e.g., narrative-driven or roguelike spins on these genres) avoid direct competition with titans.
5. **Wellness/gamified health.** $3.8B 2026 market with mainstream gaming studios largely absent — currently dominated by health-app companies rather than game studios with strong mechanic design chops.
